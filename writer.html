Rizal as a Writer

As most of us know, Jose Rizal used writing as his “sword” to fight for our country. He has written several literary works such as poems and even novels to address the issues that our country was facing back then. Some of his works that we have studied in our class are presented below:

A La Juventud Filipina

This literary piece, written in 1879, is a poem that aims to encourage the Filipino youth to take action for the betterment of the world and highlights the importance of a young person’s education and a good character. Rizal also made use of different references to other literary pieces to address this.

Read more: Memory NHCP or Studocu

El Amor Patrio

The first nationalist work of Rizal after he arrived at Europe. It is an essay that talks about the importance of understanding Philippine history and culture, as our country is beautiful in its own way and argues that love of country is a powerful force that inspires great deeds.

Read more: JSTOR

Noli Me Tangere

In 1887, Rizal published his first novel, Noli Me Tangere. It became a cornerstone of Philippine literature, exposing the injustices in our country. Crisóstomo Ibarra, a young man returning home after studying abroad, faces discrimination and abuse from those in power. This harsh reality leads him to question the system. The title, meaning "Touch me not" in Latin, hints at tragic events and his fight for justice.

Read more: Rizal's Own Story of His Life

El Filibusterismo

Rizal returned with a darker companion to Noli Me Tangere - El Filibusterismo in the year 1891. He introduces Simoun, a wealthy jeweler, who was secretly Crisóstomo Ibarra. Seeking for justice, he plots a fiery rebellion against the oppressive rule. The work explores the desperate fight for freedom, the price of revolution, and the factors that contribute to a broken society.

Ang Liham sa mga Kababaihan ng Malolos

In this essay, written in 1889, Rizal reached out to the young women of Malolos, Bulacan. Although the meaning behind his statements may be ironic and questionable, it tells them to pursue their education and be instruments of change to our country.
